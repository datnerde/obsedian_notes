# Business Frameworks

> Structured frameworks for open-ended product/business questions. Avoid blank-page answers by picking the right framework.

---

## 3C&P Framework

**Use when:** "Tell me about company X" or "Analyze this market"

| Dimension | Key Questions |
|---|---|
| **Customer** | Who are the users? What problem are they solving? Pain points? Segmentation? |
| **Company** | Mission? Revenue model? Competitive advantage? Resources & capabilities? |
| **Competition** | Who are the competitors? Market landscape? Direct vs indirect? Barriers to entry? |
| **Product** | What does it do? Core features? User journey? How does it differ from competitors? |

---

## 4P Marketing Mix

**Use when:** "How would you market X?" or "How to improve X's growth?"

| P | Focus | Questions |
|---|---|---|
| **Product** | What we sell | Features, quality, design, branding, packaging |
| **Price** | How much | Pricing strategy, discounts, payment options, value perception |
| **Place** | Where to sell | Distribution channels, online/offline, geography, logistics |
| **Promotion** | How to communicate | Advertising, PR, social media, content marketing, partnerships |

## STP Framework

**Use when:** "How would you launch a new product?" or "Who should we target?"

### 1. Segmentation

| Type | Dimensions | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Demographic** | Age, Gender, Income, Education, Occupation | College students vs working professionals |
| **Geographic** | Country, Region, City, Urban/Rural, Climate | Tier-1 cities vs rural areas |
| **Psychographic** | Lifestyle, Values, Personality, Interests | Health-conscious vs convenience-oriented |
| **Behavioral** | Usage rate, Loyalty, Purchase occasion, Benefits sought | Power users vs casual users |

### 2. Targeting
- Which segment(s) are most attractive? Consider: size, growth, profitability, accessibility
- Is the segment large enough? Can we reach them effectively?

### 3. Positioning
- How do we want the target segment to perceive us vs competitors?
- What is our unique value proposition?
- Template: *"For [target segment], our product is [category] that [key benefit] because [reason to believe]."*

## AIDA Funnel (Sales & Marketing)

| Stage | User Mindset | Key Metrics |
|---|---|---|
| **Awareness** | "I know this exists" | Impressions, Reach, Brand recall |
| **Interest** | "I want to learn more" | CTR, Page views, Time on site |
| **Desire** | "I want this" | Add to cart, Wishlist, Sign-up |
| **Action** | "I'm buying" | Conversion rate, Revenue, AOV |

---

## Vanity Metrics vs Actionable Metrics

| Vanity Metric | Actionable Metric | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Total downloads | DAU / MAU | Downloads don't mean people use the product |
| Total page views | Conversion rate | Views don't drive revenue |
| Total registered users | Activation rate | Registered ≠ Active |
| Social media followers | Engagement rate | Followers can be bots |

**Business Metric vs KPI:**
- Business Metric = measures any aspect of the business (e.g. page views, sessions)
- KPI = directly tied to company's core objective (e.g. MRR, Churn Rate)

All KPIs are metrics; not all metrics are KPIs. In interviews, explain *why* your chosen metric is a KPI, not a vanity metric.
