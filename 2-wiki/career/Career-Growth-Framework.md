# Career Growth Framework

> Distilled from career coaching. The key insight: at every level, "do the work well" is necessary but not sufficient — the visibility and relationship dimensions change as you level up.

---

## Level Progression

### Junior → Senior
- **Do the work well within your defined scope** — deliver reliably, make your outputs visible
- "Bright work" = outputs that stand out: clean, documented, impactful

### Senior → Lead/Staff
- Continue producing bright work
- **Don't make your manager look bad in front of stakeholders** — own the relationship management; your manager should be able to put you in front of anyone
- **Have budget support for your level** — the business needs to see your work as worth the investment

---

## Techniques That Accelerate Growth

### Be Proactive
- Assume no one will give you air time — take it
- Even if it feels like self-promotion, the alternative is invisibility
- Take the extra step that wasn't asked for — that's what gets remembered

### Build Business Relationships
- Stakeholder management is a craft, not a personality trait
- Reading list cue: books on emotional intelligence, influence, and communication
- Warmer tone, more empathy in cross-functional meetings

### Have Alignment Conversations With Your Manager
Run this 3-part check-in quarterly:
1. *"What are the requirements for my next level, and how do they map to my projects?"*
2. *"Where am I today relative to those requirements?"*
3. *"Here's my plan to close the gap — does this make sense to you?"*

### Be Opinionated (Correctly)
- Have a point of view — silence is often interpreted as incompetence or lack of interest
- Fight for your ideas in the room; circle back and revise if you get new information
- Being "opinionated but coachable" is the target

---

## Common Failure Modes at Senior Level

| Pattern | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| Heads-down only | Invisible to stakeholders; can't advance |
| Sycophancy | No intellectual credibility |
| Constant escalation | Manager can't trust you with stakeholders |
| Doing everything alone | Not building organizational leverage |
