# Why Leave / Why Us — Interview Narratives

> Three ready-to-use templates. Pick based on company type; fill in `[company]` and `[specific]` blanks.

---

## Template 1: Universal (Default)

**Use when:** Most companies, especially when uncertain about culture. Balances growth, stability, and fit.

> I've really appreciated my time at Circle K. Over the past few years I've been able to lead high-impact work in real estate and merchandise analytics, and I've learned a ton.
>
> At this point I feel I've grown as much as I can in the current scope, and I'm looking for bigger, more complex problems and a faster path to growth. I'm especially excited about this role at [company] because of [specific: e.g. the scale of your platform and the focus on experimentation].

---

## Template 2: Traditional / Enterprise

**Use when:** Banks, large retail, manufacturing, consulting. Emphasizes stability and long-term value.

> I'm very grateful for the foundation I've built at Circle K. I've had the chance to build scalable tools, like geospatial ROI models used by regional managers and pricing strategies across thousands of stores.
>
> Now I'd like to bring that experience into a more mature organization with clearer progression paths and a larger platform. What attracts me to [company] is the combination of long-term stability and data-driven decision-making, where I can contribute over many years and keep growing.

---

## Template 3: Tech / Startup

**Use when:** Tech companies, AI/ML startups, product-led orgs. Emphasizes iteration speed and technical depth.

> At Circle K I've been able to run end-to-end ML projects — building PySpark pipelines, running A/B tests, and even shipping RAG-style LLM tools for faster entity matching. That's been really fun and rewarding.
>
> I'm now looking for a higher-velocity tech environment where I can iterate faster, own products end-to-end, and go deeper on AI/ML. From what I've seen, [company]'s [e.g. culture of experimentation / focus on applied ML] is exactly the kind of environment where I can both contribute immediately and keep pushing my skills.

---

## Key Principle

Always end with *why this company specifically* — the answer must not feel like it could apply to any company. Reference something concrete: team structure, product area, tech stack, experimentation culture, or a recent company achievement you read about.
