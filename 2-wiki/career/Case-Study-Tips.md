# Case Study Interview Tips

> Product Sense interviews test *how you think*, not what you know. Communication style matters as much as content.

---

## What Interviewers Are Evaluating

| Criteria | What They're Looking For | How to Show It |
|---|---|---|
| **Structured Thinking** | Systematic problem decomposition | Use frameworks (6-step, AARRR, funnel decomposition) |
| **Business Acumen** | Understanding of commercial logic | Connect metrics to business goals, mention trade-offs |
| **Data Intuition** | Know where to look and what to measure | Propose specific analyses, mention segmentation, name confounders |
| **Communication** | Clear expression of reasoning | Summarize before diving deep, use "First… Second… Third…" |
| **Creativity** | Surface non-obvious hypotheses | Mention edge cases, second-order effects, counter-arguments |

---

## Communication Do's and Don'ts

### Do's
- **State framework before details:** "I'd approach this in 3 steps: first clarify the metric, then segment, finally propose hypotheses."
- **Think out loud:** Interviewers want to hear your process — silence is a deduction.
- **Clarify upfront:** "Before I dive in — by 'engagement', do you mean likes + comments + shares?"
- **Give priorities:** When listing hypotheses, say "My top hypothesis is X because..."
- **Quantify:** "If conversion drops 2pp, that's roughly \~$X revenue impact."
- **Close with a summary:** "To summarize — I identified 3 causes, my top recommendation is..."

### Don'ts
- Don't give the answer without explaining your reasoning
- Don't lead with "I would run an A/B test" before diagnosing the problem
- Don't list metrics without explaining why you chose each one
- Don't forget guardrail metrics
- Don't ignore edge cases and trade-offs

---

## 30-Minute Time Allocation

| Phase | Time | What to Do |
|---|---|---|
| Clarify & Restate | ~3 min | Ask questions, restate problem in your own words |
| Structure & Framework | ~5 min | Lay out your approach, get alignment |
| Deep Dive & Analysis | ~15 min | Walk through framework, segment, decompose, hypothesize |
| Recommend & Summarize | ~5 min | Clear recommendation with trade-offs |
| Q&A | ~2 min | Answer follow-ups, acknowledge limitations |

---

## Starter Phrases

**Opening:** "Great question. Let me structure my approach — I'll start by clarifying the metric, then segment the data, and finally propose hypotheses."

**Transition:** "That's one possibility, but let me also consider an alternative explanation..."

**Quantifying:** "To put this in perspective, if we assume X users and Y conversion rate, the impact would be roughly Z..."

**Closing:** "To summarize — the most likely root cause is X, I'd recommend we validate this by Y, and if confirmed, the action plan would be Z."

---

## Handling Common Follow-Ups

| Follow-up | How to Handle |
|---|---|
| "What if your hypothesis is wrong?" | Have a Plan B: "If data doesn't support X, I'd investigate Y and Z" |
| "How would you prioritize?" | Impact × Feasibility matrix; mention short-term vs long-term |
| "What data would you need?" | Be specific: "I'd need a daily time series of X, segmented by Y, joined with Z" |
| "What are the trade-offs?" | Always acknowledge: "The trade-off is between A (short-term gain) and B (long-term risk)" |
| "Can you go deeper?" | Pick ONE hypothesis and drill: data needed → analysis plan → expected outcome → action |
