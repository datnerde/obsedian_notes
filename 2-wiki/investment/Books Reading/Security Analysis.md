# Security Analysis — Graham & Dodd

## Core Idea
Investing is not a hard science — both skill and chance play a role. Security analysis is the discipline of **maximizing the influence of skill and minimizing that of chance** through rigorous fundamental analysis.

> *"Security Analysis is their prescription for maximizing the influence of the former and minimizing that of the latter."*

---

## Key Concepts

### Intrinsic Value
> *"Intrinsic value is an elusive concept. In general terms it is understood to be that value which is justified by the facts — assets, earnings, dividends, definite prospects — as distinct from market quotations established by artificial manipulation or distorted by psychological excesses."*

- Intrinsic value is **not** a precise number — it's a range
- The analyst's job is not to calculate it exactly but to determine whether **price is materially below or above** it
- Modern view: **intrinsic value is determined by earning power**, not just asset value

> *"The essential point is that security analysis does not seek to determine exactly what is the intrinsic value of a given security. It needs only to establish either that the value is adequate — e.g., to protect a bond or to justify a stock purchase."*

### Estimating Future Earnings — Two Vital Rules
1. **Stability preference**: companies with stable earnings are easier to forecast and thus preferable; the more volatile a firm's earnings, the further back you should look (Graham & Dodd: look back 10 years)
2. **Average over trend**: a firm's **average earnings** provide a rough guide to the future; the earnings **trend** is far less reliable as a predictor

> *"A firm's average earnings can provide a rough guide to the future; the earnings trend is far less reliable."*

### Red Flag: Income vs Cash Flow Divergence
> *"When the income statement and the cash flow statement start to diverge, it's a signal that something is amiss."*

Companies can report earnings but not generate cash — this divergence is a classic warning sign of earnings manipulation or deteriorating business quality.

### Double-Counting Risk
> *"Graham and Dodd referred to this as 'double-counting' — investors buy the stock on the basis of their faith in management and then, seeing that the stock has risen, take it as additional proof of management's powers and bid the stock up further."*

Don't use rising price as evidence of quality. Price confirms your thesis only if your thesis was formed independently of price.

### Three Obstacles to Analyst Success
1. **Inadequate or Incorrect Data** — garbage in, garbage out
2. **Uncertainties of the Future** — even good analysis can't predict macro shocks
3. **Irrational Behavior of the Market** — price may not converge to value on your timeline

> *"By the time the price finally does reflect the value, this value may have changed considerably."*

---

## Graham's Philosophy vs Modern Finance
- Modern finance: markets are (mostly) efficient; price = value
- Graham & Dodd: Mr. Market is irrational in the short term; price ≠ value; skill + patience wins
- Both can be true: markets are **mostly** efficient but mispricings exist and can be exploited by disciplined analysts
