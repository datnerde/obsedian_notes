# Options Trading

> Personal notes on options mechanics and personal rules. Not a complete options primer — assumes familiarity with calls/puts.

---

## Key Observations

### Buying Long-dated Calls (LEAPS)
- **Lower liquidity** — bid-ask spreads are wider on far-dated options; factor this into entry/exit cost
- **Contrarian timing helps** — best bought when the stock has sold off heavily and implied vol is high (everyone is bearish); the premium is expensive in absolute terms but the directional bet is better
- **The "roll"** — if stock hasn't moved, roll the call to a later expiry rather than taking the loss; but only if the thesis is still valid

### General Options Rules
- **Position sizing**: max 10% of portfolio in options at any time
- **Close early** — time decay (theta) accelerates in the last 30 days; don't let a profitable directional bet die of theta
- **Hedge** — naked long calls are fine; naked short options need hedging (spreads, or holding the underlying)
- **Don't trade on policy news** — expected/unexpected binary events destroy options positions

---

## When to Buy Calls (Personal Checklist)
1. Stock has pulled back ≥ 20% from recent high
2. Company fundamentals unchanged (earnings power intact)
3. Bull case still valid but market is fearful
4. IV rank is not extreme (avoid buying after a spike in implied vol)
5. Expiry ≥ 3 months out to allow thesis to play out

---

## Options vs. Stock Decision
- **Use options when**: you want leverage, or when you want to express a view with limited downside (max loss = premium)
- **Use stock when**: you have high conviction and can tolerate mark-to-market volatility without being shaken out; time is unlimited
