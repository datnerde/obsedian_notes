# Stock Valuation Framework

> Practical valuation playbook by stock category. The goal: estimate intrinsic value to avoid overpaying and identify when a quality company is on sale.

---

## By Stock Category

### Large-cap Tech
- **PE ratio**: years to recoup purchase price at current earnings
  - If growth is high, forward PE will compress → stock looks expensive but isn't
- **Graham formula (simplified DCF)**: `PE ≈ g × 2 + (10–20)` where `g` = estimated 10-yr net profit growth rate
  - Use 10 when rates are high, 20 when rates are low
- **Check PE history chart** — buy below historical average PE

### Small-cap Growth
- **PS ratio**: `market cap / revenue` — compare to industry peers
- Project 3–5 year forward PE: if current growth continues, what will earnings look like?
- **Key signal**: falling growth rate → exit

### Financials
- **PB ratio**: `price / book value per share`
- Compare to historical average PB — financials are cyclical, so mean reversion matters

### Consumer / Stable Growth
- **PE** (primary): use analyst consensus EPS to compute current PE
- **ROE > 20%** = strong competitive position worth paying a premium for
  - `ROE = Net Income / Average Shareholders' Equity`

---

## Buy & Sell Rules

### Blue-chip Moat Stocks
- **Buy trigger**: pullback ≥ 20% from recent high
- **Valuation check**: PE < 25 (don't overpay even for quality)
- **Sell**: almost never — only if moat disappears (business model disrupted, pricing power lost)

### High-growth Small Caps
- PE cutoff matters less — focus on growth rate trajectory
- Project future net income; buy when priced for pessimism
- **Sell**: when growth rate starts declining meaningfully

### "Cigar Butt" Value Stocks
- **PE < 15** (cheap on earnings)
- **PB < 1** (trading below liquidation value)
- **Dividend yield ≥ 3%** (getting paid to wait)
- No specific hold target — exit after 40–50% appreciation or if thesis breaks

---

## Moat Assessment Checklist

- Brand power — can they charge a premium?
- Differentiation — is the product unique / hard to replicate?
- Pricing / raise-price ability — have they historically raised prices without losing customers?
- Corporate culture — retention, innovation, mission clarity
- Switching costs — how painful is it for customers to leave?
