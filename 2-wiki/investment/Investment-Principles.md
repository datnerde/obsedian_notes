# Personal Investment Principles

> Hard-won rules from trading experience. Intended as a checklist before acting, not a rigid algorithm.

---

## Core Rules

1. **Watch ETF premiums** — when buying ETFs, check the premium/discount to NAV, especially for niche or leveraged ETFs
2. **Set stop-loss and take-profit levels before entering** — define exit criteria upfront, not in the heat of the moment
3. **Leveraged products are for short-term trending markets only** — not suitable for long-term holding; use fast in / fast out
4. **Options amplify swings, but time decay is a cost** — close positions early; don't let theta bleed kill a good directional bet
5. **Hedge options positions** — naked options require careful risk management
6. **Don't trade on policy speculation** — the risk/reward is poor; policy outcomes are unpredictable even when direction seems obvious
7. **Keep investment and speculation accounts separate** — investment should be 80–90% of total; don't let a bad speculative trade contaminate the core portfolio
8. **Media-driven information accelerates cycles** — when everyone in social media is saying the same thing, it's already priced in; that's the exit signal
9. **If it sounds too good to be true, it isn't** — no exceptions
10. **Bad news = buying opportunity if fundamentals are intact** — e.g., Crowdstrike outage, PDD controversies: the company's earning power didn't change; the market overreacted

---

## Meta-principle: Separate Alpha from Beta

- **Investment account** (~80–90%): buy-and-hold moat businesses and broad index ETFs
- **Speculation account** (~10–20%): options, short-term trades, higher-risk positions
- Never let a speculative loss exceed the drawdown that would affect sleep or decision-making
